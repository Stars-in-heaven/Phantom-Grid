// 01 — Screw Identification
Hinge Screws.

Started by physically measuring the hinge screws with a caliper. Identified as a self-tapping countersunk screw — pointed tip cuts its own thread into the print, no pre-tapped hole needed.

Property Measured Value Notes
Shank diameter2.6mmThreaded shaft
Head diameter5.1mmFlat countersunk top
Total length15.2mmTip to top of head
Head typeCountersunkSits flush when driven in
Screw typeSelf-tappingPointed tip, cuts own thread
Screws were ultimately too large to fit the enclosure. Hinge attachment method was changed — see Section 3.
15.2mm is long for a small hinge. When choosing filament, PETG handles self-tapping screws better than PLA — PLA can crack under thread-cutting stress.

// 03 — Hinge Design
Printed Barrel Hinge.

The original leaf hinge screws were too large for the enclosure. Decision made to ditch separate hinge hardware entirely and design a barrel hinge directly into the print. The case spine becomes the hinge — no external components at all.

"The knuckles from each side alternate and slot into each other. One continuous pin runs through the whole block top to bottom. Nothing to source, nothing to buy."

Layout
  • Two hinge points along the spine — one near top, one near middle
  • 3 knuckles per hinge point — alternating between left and right panels
  • 4-wire hinge cable routed via a channel cut along the outside of the spine between the two hinges
  • Pin retained by U-bend pocket + epoxy — self-retaining, cannot slide out
Pin — Stiff Safety Pin Wire

Large safety pin, main straight shaft only. Not the coil end.

// 04 — Enclosure Split
Bottom Access.

Three split options were considered. Bottom split chosen — hidden from the use orientation, full interior access, and the main body remains one continuous rigid piece.

OptionVerdictReason
Top split Rejected Screws visible next to displays. Risks cracking near screen cutouts over time.
Back split Considered Clean exterior but complicates cable access and battery replacement.
Bottom split Chosen Hidden during use. Full interior access. Strongest main body structure.
Prototype Fastening Approach

Rather than modelling screw bosses at this stage — print the case, drill pilot holes by hand, drive the self-tapping screws in manually. Faster for a first prototype and the results will inform the next revision.

Use a 2mm drill bit for 2.6mm self-tapping screws — gives the thread material to bite into without splitting the print.
Drill slowly — high speed melts PLA/PETG instead of cutting cleanly.
If the print cracks near a hole, the wall is too thin — thicken it in the next revision.
Minimum 1.5mm wall on each side of the split edge. Minimum 2mm bottom plate thickness to prevent flex.
Leave extra cable slack inside — battery access means opening the case without stressing the hinge cable.
Assembly Order
  • Install displays through front/top cutouts first
  • Route hinge cable through spine channel before closing
  • Feed PCBs in through the bottom last
  • Check largest component clears the bottom opening before committing to final split dimensions
